The Pareto MBA Experience

We have condensed a traditional MBA curriculum into an 8-week program using a scientific approach with microlearning, spaced repetition, case studies and exercises at its core. Study at your own pace for 2-3 hours per week, then go deeper together with our teachers and your fellow course participants in a weekly Zoom classroom session. We continuously update our program to include the latest business cases and technologies, such as generative AI and dealing with business slowdown and adversity.

  • Business owners

  • Business developers and advisors

    You want a better toolkit for analysing and improving businesses that you work with as an investor or advisor.

Our curriculum

Week 1: Basic Business Skills and Tools

You will learn key concepts and business language, how to use basic business tools and why some businesses are better than others.

Week 2: Business Models, Resources and Strategy

You will learn how to evaluate business opportunities, compare business models and communicate an opinion on vision and strategy.

Week 3: Marketing and Sales

You will learn how to approach the market with a product or service and how to think about niches, customer segments, branding and positioning.

Week 4: Growth, Expansion and Finance

You will learn how businesses grow and how to lead a growing organization, get familiar with scalability, unit economics and how to finance growth.

Week 5: Leadership and Communication

You will learn how to lead yourself and your team, and how to communicate your ideas clearly.

Week 6: Management, Operations and Culture

You will learn how companies are structured and operate in the real world, how to organize and measure work, and to prioritize and delegate tasks.

Week 7: Adversity and Change

You will learn to detect signs of trouble, how to deal with tough times both personally and as an organization, and how to think about change.

Week 8: Finance, Acquisitions and Synergies

You will learn the basics of buying and selling companies, including valuation, types of deals and what an acquisition process can look like.

A word from the founders

Why did we start this?

Why did you start Pareto Business School?

Both Jens and Mathias studied business at university and at leading MBA schools in the US and UK, respectively. Having then become entrepreneurs and later investors, both felt that there was a clear need for an updated and accessible business program. Traditional MBAs at top schools are great but are very expensive and require a lot of time, which puts them out of reach for most people. At the same time, technology and business models are changing fast, and traditional programs can't keep up. Our solution is an online program based on case studies from companies on the frontline of technology and business models, delivered with a no-nonsense and pragmatic approach. We also feel that a lot of people are struggling in their business lives, be it as part of a big corporation or a startup or as a creator, and that there's a lot of best practice, tricks, tips, tools and models that could make life easier for them.

What makes the Pareto MBA different?

The fundamental philosophy of Pareto Business School is to provide the maximum amount of practical skills and knowledge over time with a minimum of time invested. To achieve this we focus on terminology, playbooks and practical tools from real-world settings, and try to avoid being too academic. That said, we are strong believers in making sense of the world through mental models and frameworks, since it's much more efficient to learn model thinking than to try to memorize disconnected information. This is also why we use case studies, but not in the way traditional MBA programs would - our case studies are shorter and to the point, and also updated as we go along, to make sure you always have the freshest real-life business examples to relate to. During the program, we will also discuss real-world business events as they unfold, and relate them to models and frameworks you learn as part of the program.
